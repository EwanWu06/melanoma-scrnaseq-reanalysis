## Citation

Tsoi J, et al. **Multi-stage Differentiation Defines Melanoma Subtypes with
Differential Vulnerability to Drug-Induced Iron-Dependent Oxidative Stress.**
*Cancer Cell* 33(5):890–904.e5 (2018). DOI: 10.1016/j.ccell.2018.03.017

## 1. Data Source

*Which data revealed the four states — cell lines or patient tumors?*

- **Initial discovery: 53 melanoma cell lines** (bulk RNA-seq) — GSE80829.
  The four states were first identified by clustering this cell-line cohort.
- **Validation:** patient tumor cohorts (e.g. **TCGA**) were used to confirm
  that the same four states are detectable in primary patient tumor data.

## 2. The Four-State Model

Ordered from **most → least differentiated**:

| State | MITF | SOX10 | NGFR | AXL | Differentiation |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Melanocytic** | ↑ | ↑ | ↓ | ↓ | most differentiated |
| **Transitory** | ↑ | ↑ | ↑ | ↓ | intermediate–high |
| **Neural crest-like** | ↓ | ↑ | ↑ | ↑ | low |
| **Undifferentiated** | ↓ | ↓ | ↓ | ↑ | least differentiated |

## 3. Biological Interpretation

- The four states form a continuous **dedifferentiation trajectory**.
- Melanoma cells exhibit **phenotypic plasticity**: under therapeutic
  pressure they can regress along this trajectory toward the
  undifferentiated end, thereby **escaping treatment**.
- Phenotypic plasticity is thus framed as a mechanism of treatment
  resistance, rather than resistance arising solely from fixed mutations.

## 4. Clinical Implications

- The model exposes a **new vulnerability of resistant cells**: the more
  dedifferentiated states (**Neural crest-like** and **Undifferentiated**)
  are markedly **more sensitive to ferroptosis** (iron-dependent oxidative
  cell death).
- This suggests **combining ferroptosis inducers with targeted therapy** to
  eliminate cells that escape via dedifferentiation-driven resistance.

## 5. Relevance to Our Project

- This is our **biological ground-truth framework**.
- We use these four markers (**MITF, SOX10, NGFR, AXL**) to assign
  Tsoi-state labels to the clusters identified by each computational method
  (classical and deep-learning).
- **Caveat:** the four states were derived from **cell lines**, not patient
  tumors. Whether the same states are cleanly recoverable in *patient*
  scRNA-seq is exactly part of our research question — Balderson 2024 report
  yes (with a classical workflow); our Stage 2 found only a partial recovery
  (see `docs/stage2_report.md`), and Stage 3 re-validates this with
  deep-learning methods.
